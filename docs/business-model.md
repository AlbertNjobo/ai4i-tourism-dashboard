# Business Model Summary

| Field | Response |
|-------|----------|
| **Problem** | Tourism authorities lack a unified, interactive view of visitor trends, spend, and service quality across destinations, leading to reactive resource allocation. |
| **Primary user** | Tourism authority analysts, destination managers, local business association leads |
| **Beneficiary** | Zimbabwean tourism sector — better-informed decisions improve visitor experience and revenue |
| **Customer or payer** | POTRAZ / Ministry of Tourism (institutional licensing), or tourism boards directly |
| **Value proposition** | Reduces time from data to decision from weeks to minutes; identifies revenue leakage and service gaps before they compound |
| **Revenue or funding model** | Institutional licensing (annual subscription per tourism board/authority), or donor/programme-funded public-good model |
| **Cost drivers** | Hosting (Vercel/Netlify), data updates, support, training, future AI integration |
| **Partnerships** | Zimbabwe Tourism Authority, Zimbabwe Parks and Wildlife Management Authority, local hospitality associations |
| **Pilot market** | Victoria Falls tourism cluster — highest visitor volume and spend concentration |
| **Adoption risks** | Institutional inertia, data freshness concerns, digital literacy of end users |
| **Success metrics** | Dashboard adoption rate, decision turnaround time, visitor satisfaction scores, revenue per destination |
